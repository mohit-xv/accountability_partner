      🧘 60-second rescue kit

      For the moment the urge to quit — or scroll — hits. Pick one. Each takes about a minute.

      🌊 Urge surfing

      Don't fight the urge — watch it. Name it out loud ("I want to check my phone"). Notice where it sits in your body. Breathe slowly and watch it like a wave: it rises, peaks… and passes in about 90 seconds. You don't have to obey it — just outlast it.

      💪 Muscle reset (PMR)

      Clench your fists hard for 5 seconds — release. Shoulders up to your ears, 5 seconds — release. Jaw tight, 5 — release. Legs, 5 — release. One pass drains the physical charge that anxiety rides on.

      ⚡ Rename the nerves

      Say it out loud: "I'm not anxious — I'm ready." Same racing heart, same alert brain; one label freezes you, the other fuels you. Then do the smallest possible rep: open the book, one problem, two minutes.

      📜 The science Accountability Partner is built on

      Long preparations (exams, big goals) follow three psychological phases. This app — and your AI partner — are designed around them.

      Phase 1 · High motivation (first ~30%)

      Dopamine, novelty and external pressure do the pushing. The job here: bank reps and build the habit while motivation is cheap.

      Phase 2 · The grind (~30–75%)

      Motivation dips — the goal feels far away (psychological distancing), progress feels invisible (perceived vs. actual learning), and comparing yourself to others drains you. The daily log and streak exist for exactly this phase: trust the record, not the feeling.

      Phase 3 · The final push (last ~25%)

      Anxiety rises because the goal matters but self-belief wobbles. The Yerkes–Dodson law says moderate arousal is peak performance — so the app reframes nerves as readiness, not threat.

      The engine: Self-Determination Theory

      Autonomy — you choose your quests; SMART, finishable-today tasks beat vague plans (and beat hyperbolic discounting, the brain's bias for instant rewards). Competence — active recall, a mistake register in your end-of-day notes, and sleep for memory consolidation. Relatedness — an accountability partner who is honest with you, plus self-compassion after slips instead of shame spirals.

      Managing the hard days

      Friction design for distractions, urge surfing for cravings, progressive muscle relaxation and exercise for stress — and relabeling anxiety as excitement when the day comes.
